# Split My Bill — Product Overview

---

## The One-Line Truth

Split My Bill is a free, frictionless tool that splits any restaurant bill — from a photo, a voice note, or a quick message — in seconds, with no account, no app, no paywall.

---

## Vision & Philosophy

Most bill-splitting tools make a simple problem complicated. They ask you to sign up. They want your credit card. They launch into dashboards, group setups, and "premium plans." By the time you've finished the onboarding, everyone at the table has already done the math in their heads and given up.

We believe the opposite: **the best tool is the one that gets out of your way.**

Split My Bill is built around a few core convictions:

- **Free, actually.** No trials, no freemium tiers, no "basic is free, everything useful costs money." You snap a photo, you get an answer. That's it.
- **No friction, no signup.** No account. No registration. No "download the app first." Open Telegram, find the bot, send your bill. Done.
- **Natural to use.** You talk to it like you'd talk to a friend: *"Split this for three people"* or *"Alice had the pasta and Bob had the steak."* It understands.
- **Just do the job well.** One job. Do it right. Every time.

The product is a tool, not a platform. It has no social feed, no user profiles, no friend lists. It exists only to answer one question: *"How much does everyone owe?"*

---

## What It Does

Split My Bill reads a photo of any restaurant bill and splits it however you need — equally, by item, or by any custom arrangement you describe.

You interact with it through natural language. You don't fill out forms or tap through menus. You just say what you want, the same way you'd ask a person.

**Examples of requests it handles:**

- *"Split equally for 4 people"*
- *"Alice had the Caesar salad and the wine. Bob had the steak. Split the rest equally."*
- *"What's the total after 20% tip?"*
- *"How much did just the appetizers cost?"*
- *"Divide everything except the desserts"*

It reads the bill, understands the request, and returns a clear per-person breakdown — no ambiguity, no rounding confusion.

---

## How People Use It

### The Core Flow

1. **Send a photo** of a restaurant bill (paper receipt, screen, handwritten — anything legible)
2. **Describe how to split it** — in the photo caption, as a follow-up message, or as a voice note
3. **Get an answer** in seconds

That's the entire interaction. No back-and-forth setup. The photo stays active for 30 minutes, so you can ask follow-up questions or adjust the split after seeing the first answer.

### Input Flexibility

Split My Bill meets people where they are. You can interact with it however is most natural in the moment:

| You send | It works |
|---|---|
| A photo with a text caption | Answer comes immediately |
| A photo first, then a text message | It waits for your request, then responds |
| A photo, then a **voice message** | It transcribes your words and responds — no typing required |
| Just a text question about a bill you already sent | It remembers the photo from the last 30 minutes |

Voice support is a key differentiator. When you're at a table with friends, typing is awkward. Speaking is natural. Split My Bill handles voice natively — no separate step, no transcription service to configure.

---

## Where It Lives

**Telegram** is the primary interface. Telegram is available on every platform (iOS, Android, desktop, web), requires no app store download beyond Telegram itself, and has zero friction to start a conversation with a bot. It's the ideal lightweight delivery mechanism for a tool like this.

A **web API** also exists for integration into other surfaces (such as a mobile-first web app with a voice-first UI). The architecture is designed to support multiple frontends without changing the core logic.

---

## What Makes It Different

Most tools in this space exist on a spectrum between "too simple" (a basic calculator that just divides a total by N) and "too complex" (an app that requires creating a group, adding contacts, itemizing every dish, and settling up via payment integration).

Split My Bill sits in a third position: **intelligent but invisible.**

It handles the actual hard cases — unequal splits, custom item assignments, partial splits, tax and tip calculations — without making you do any of the work of setting them up. The AI reads the bill and understands your request. You don't manage the data; you just state your intent.

**Key differentiators:**

- **Photo-first.** Other calculators require manual entry. We read the bill directly.
- **Voice-first.** No other tool in this category lets you just speak your request.
- **No account required.** Zero barrier to first use.
- **Free with no ceiling.** No features locked behind a plan.
- **Conversational.** You can refine, adjust, and ask follow-ups naturally.
- **Any split, any complexity.** Works for a 2-person lunch or a 12-person dinner with custom orders.

## Who Uses This

Split My Bill is for anyone who has ever been at a restaurant table trying to figure out who owes what. That's nearly everyone, regularly.

**Primary scenarios:**

- Friends splitting a dinner, some of whom ordered more than others
- Couples on work trips splitting a meal for expense reports
- Groups at restaurants where the server won't split the check
- Anyone who just wants a fast, correct answer without spreadsheets or drama

There is no niche demographic here. The use case is universal and recurs constantly in everyday life.

## Tone & Personality

The product is **calm, competent, and unpretentious.**

It doesn't celebrate itself. It doesn't add flourishes. It gives you the answer and gets out of the way. When it talks, it's brief and clear — like a knowledgeable friend who doesn't waste words.

It is not:
- Cutesy or emoji-heavy
- Formal or corporate
- Salesy or upsell-minded

It is:
- Direct
- Accurate
- Effortlessly helpful

The product's personality in one sentence: *"Here's the answer. You're welcome."*

## What "Free" Really Means Here

"Free" is not a marketing tactic. It's a design constraint. The product is built to do one job well, for everyone, without asking anything in return. There are no:

- Hidden costs that appear after you're invested
- Premium tiers that gate the useful features
- Ads embedded in responses
- Data collection or resale
- "Pro" versions with better accuracy

The tool works the same for everyone. The answer you get is as good as the answer anyone gets. That's the promise.

---

## Marketing & Positioning Notes

**The core message is ease, not features.** When people talk about Split My Bill to others, they'll say *"it's so easy"* before they say anything about what it can do. Lead with the experience, not the feature list.

**The voice input is underappreciated and highly compelling.** Talking to an assistant at a table feels futuristic but natural. It's worth highlighting in visual and campaign work.

**The "no account" aspect removes the biggest drop-off moment.** In a world where every tool requires sign-up, "just open Telegram and use it" is genuinely surprising. That surprise should be part of the story.

**Accuracy matters enormously.** People get genuinely stressed about money fairness at tables. The product being *right* — not approximately right, but exactly right — builds trust and drives word-of-mouth. Any creative work should convey precision and reliability, not approximation.

**The real competition is mental math and peer pressure.** Nobody wants to be the person who does the math wrong, or the person who makes everyone do extra steps to figure it out. Split My Bill removes that social friction. That emotional truth is the real story.
